Mission

Streamlining the takeout ordering process by providing an app based ordering and payment solution, as well as a secure box to keep food at an appropriate temperature.

Product: App and Temperature-Controlled Box Prototype

SPRINT 1

This first sprint was all about seeing whether there was interest in our service. Simple paper prototypes were created to guide user testers and a pizza box was used in place of our box prototype. Using pizza as an incentive, we gave brief interviews to each user-tester where we uncovered positive feedback as well as uncovering concerns moving forward with this product.

SPRINT 2

These weeks were sprint iterating out paper prototype and upgrading our box prototyping to include the main feature of "automaticity." The post-test interviews were directed at simplifying the user interface and user experience as this sprint was directed on the ordering process.

SPRINT 3

Started with simple paper prototypes, user-tested and iterated upon to eventual designing of the user interface and compiling user-flows. These wireframes done in Figma(collaboration based barebones, cloud illustrator) and eventually moved to InVision for a Low-Fidelity HotSpot prototype. These were presented on mobile devices and features were added and improved based off user feedback.

SPRINT 4

Now was the time to take our solution outside into the world. We instigated three separate cases where users had to order, travel, and pickup from the "ChowBox" at the desired restaurant location. Thorough interviews were conducted based on the user-testers experience where the product resounded with positive responses.
